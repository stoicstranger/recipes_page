The old fashioned, surely the most elevated cocktail in this brief list, bares all it has in a small but multi-faceted pour that caters to those among us who can afford the bourbon that makes it so.

To make an old-fashioned, you'll need to gather the following ingredients in a dimly lit, leatherbound room:

  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 1 teaspoon water
  • 2 ounces bourbon (brand deliberately unspecified)
  • Garnish: orange peel

The steps are as follows:

  1. Add sugar and bitters to a glass
  2. Add water and stir until sugar is nearly dissolved
  3. Fill with ice and add bourbon, stirring gently
  4. Squeeze the oil of the orange peel over the glass and place peel into glass
  5. Put your turtle neck on if it wasn't already, start the jazz, and pray your friend recommended the right bourbon
